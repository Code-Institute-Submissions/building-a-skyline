This area is reserved for players who know how game works but want to know more about advanced mechanics. Here you can go through some advanced tutorials and helpfull links. And, of course - basics of traffic.

Zones:

This game has 4 major zones and number of sub-zones:

RESIDENTIAL COMMERCIAL INDUSTRY OFFICE

This is known in game as RCI

Please note that Office zone isn't available right at the begining, so you will have to spend some time to open it in game. If you play with everything unlocked, it will be a major zone

Specializations:

Each zone has "sub-zones" which divide the major zones into high and low density. There are also specializations for each zone type:

  • Residential: Self-sufficient
  • Commercial: Organic and Local Produce, Tourism, Leisure
  • Industrial: Farming, Forestry, Oil and Ore
  • Office: IT cluster

    • Each of these have their uses, and depending on your preferences, you can design a city to your liking.

So by now, you heard about something called mods. This section will help you to select some basic mods that every Skyline planner should have in his/hers collection:

Steam Workshop page:

This will be the most visited page, once you start using mods. The reason why Steam service is the most desirable platform to buy Cities: Skylines on, is that it's the ONLY platform that offers mod support. At the moment of making this page, no other platform has it. Also, mods aren't supported on consoles.

Cities:Skylines Steam workshop page
Most popular mods:

Workshop has thousands of mods but these 5 will be the most essential. Please make sure you check the date mod came out, as some of the mods are obsolete and some can even break your savegames, including hours of progress lost.

Very important thing is to READ THE DESCRIPTION OD THE MOD BEFORE you subscribe to it. This will save you a lot of embarassement and time going through support forums. And don't forget to show some love to the people who's work you are using.

Mod name: Mod description:
MoveIt! Enables you to freely move items in game, including parts of roads. It is highly recommended to have anarchy type of mod too.
Fine Road Anarchy Despite it's name, it gives you more controll by removing colision restrictions and lets you build more appealing network structures
Traffic Manager: President Edition You can set parking restrictions, lane management, speed restrictions and much more that isn't included in base game but it should.
Loading screen mod Lowers you computer RAM usage by using the same texture for multiple assets. It also lets you know about any missing or duplicate assets.
Find it It lets you find that asset you need with a handy search bar. It also has assets organised by groups, so if you can't remeber the name, you can browse the whole group.

There are thousands of mods out there, but these 5 will serve you well at any time. Without these you won't be able to progress to the section below...

Building a real-life city district using Cities: Skylines

Capital of Sweden used Cities: Skylines for their initiative to make a substantial urban redevelopment in their city. Stockholms city officials partnered with Swedish Building Service and gathered real world city planers, game developers and members of the public to discuss future development of their city.
